I am a PhD candidate in Social Epidemiology at the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health. I am also part of the [Robert Wood Johnson Foundation's Health Policy Research Scholars Program](https://healthpolicyresearch-scholars.org/scholars/dougie-zubizarreta/)

**I plan to graduate in Spring 2027 and am currently on the 2026-2027 academic job market.**

My work focuses on quantitative methods for intersectionality research (including I‑MAIHDA) and examining structural determinants of population health and health inequities across the life‑course. I have published 32 papers (16 first-author, 16 co-author), including in top journals such as *American Journal of Public Health*, *American Journal of Epidemiology*, *Social Science and Medicine*, and *Annals of Epidemiology*. 

My dissertation research focuses on applications and extensions of Intersectional Multilevel Analysis of Individual Heterogeneity and Discriminatory Accuracy (I-MAIHDA) to investigate the patterning and magnitude of intersectional sleep duration inequities across the life-course and their structural drivers.

Outside of academia, I love to run (long distance, trails), cook vegan food, and read non‑fiction.
